# LearnPuddle Product Site UX Audit

Date: 2026-05-12
Reviewer stance: UI/UX lead, with input from design, content, and asset direction.

## Product Read

LearnPuddle is not just a school LMS. The stronger product idea in the repo is:

> LearnPuddle is a branded training operating system for schools, enterprises, academies, and distributed teams. AI helps teams turn content into courses, assessments, captions, search, insights, and measurable progress.

The current product site correctly points toward a horizontal training platform, but it still sells mostly "training operations and compliance." That is credible, but it underplays the warmer, more differentiated promise: education and teaching across industries has evolved in the AI era to become easier, more fun, more adaptive, and more insightful.

## Main Diagnosis

The site has a good skeleton, but its creative layer is still quiet. It explains that LearnPuddle can manage training. It does not yet make the visitor feel: "This is what modern teaching becomes when AI is embedded into the workflow."

The repo has richer product substance than the site currently shows: AI course generation, transcript-to-quiz, semantic search, RAG chatbot, adaptive video captions, gamification, certifications, multi-tenant branding, and vertical templates. Those should become visible product moments.

## Asset Direction

The next page iteration needs owned visuals, not generic dashboard sketches. Build a small asset system that shows learning becoming interactive.

### Asset 1: Living Course Builder

Purpose: Hero/product proof.

Visual: A course-builder screen where a PDF/video/playbook flows into modules, auto-generated quizzes, captions, and a certificate path.

Composition:

- Left: uploaded source content cards: video, PDF, SOP, presentation.
- Center: AI transformation panel with "Generate course", "Create quiz", "Summarize", "Translate".
- Right: course pathway with modules, quiz, discussion, certificate.

Tone: Bright, precise, optimistic. It should look like real software, not a mystical AI graphic.

Copy labels:

- `Upload`
- `Generate`
- `Teach`
- `Track`
- `Certify`

### Asset 2: Learning Insight Map

Purpose: Explain why LearnPuddle is more insightful than old LMS tools.

Visual: A dashboard map showing completion, skill gaps, engagement, deadlines, quiz performance, and intervention suggestions across roles.

Moments to show:

- Principal/admin sees departments at risk.
- Teacher/trainer sees who needs help.
- Learner sees streak, badge, next best lesson.

Copy labels:

- `3 cohorts need reminders`
- `Skill gap: assessment design`
- `91% completion`
- `Auto-generated evidence report`

### Asset 3: AI Classroom Conversation

Purpose: Make "talks back" literal.

Visual: A lesson player with transcript, captions, chatbot, and quiz generation side panel.

Moments to show:

- Video playback with synced transcript.
- "Ask this lesson" chat.
- "Generate 5 quiz questions."
- "Explain this for frontline staff / teachers / managers."

Copy labels:

- `Ask the lesson`
- `Jump to moment`
- `Generate quiz`
- `Simplify explanation`

### Asset 4: Vertical Template Gallery

Purpose: Communicate "Shopify for training."

Visual: Template cards for Education, Corporate L&D, Healthcare, Manufacturing, Hospitality, Academies.

Each card should show:

- audience language
- program examples
- metrics tracked
- launch checklist

Example card copy:

Education:
`CPD hours, IB certifications, faculty development, department reports.`

Manufacturing:
`Safety SOPs, machine readiness, supervisor sign-off, recertification cycles.`

Corporate:
`Onboarding, compliance, sales enablement, role-based certifications.`

### Asset 5: Branded Tenant Worlds

Purpose: Show multi-tenancy and custom branding.

Visual: Three mini browser windows with different brand themes and vocabulary:

- School portal
- Fitness/gym training portal
- Corporate academy

The point: same platform, different world.

## Site Structure Recommendation

The current structure is useful, but the story should be reordered around transformation:

1. Hero: AI-era LMS promise, clear template CTA.
2. Product proof: source content becomes course, quiz, captions, insights.
3. Template gallery: schools, corporate, healthcare, manufacturing, hospitality, academies.
4. Role outcomes: admin, guide, learner, observer.
5. Analytics/proof: completion, skill gaps, reports, certifications.
6. Trust: security, tenancy, SSO, audit, roadmap.
7. Demo: tailored walkthrough.

## Highest-Impact UI Changes

1. Replace the current hero dashboard mockup with the "Living Course Builder" asset.
2. Add a template gallery directly after the hero, before generic platform capabilities.
3. Make AI workflows explicit: transcript-to-quiz, document-to-course, ask-a-lesson, semantic search, translation, captions.
4. Upgrade the logo system. The current circular logo is too low legibility at navbar size.
5. Reduce the compliance-heavy feel by adding delight: progress, badges, lesson chat, faster authoring, guided next steps.
6. Add a product screenshot strip or faux-real UI panels using existing app concepts, not generic charts only.
7. Fix local product-site title/theme leakage: platform landing rendered with `Demo School Local - LMS` as the page title in local inspection.

## Product Website Verdict

The foundation is good enough to ship a credible early website. To become memorable, it needs a stronger LearnPuddle-owned visual system and a more direct AI-era learning narrative.

The next design target should be:

> Warm enterprise trust plus visible AI learning magic.

That means fewer abstract platform claims, more product moments: "upload this", "AI builds that", "learner experiences this", "admin sees this", "organization proves this."
